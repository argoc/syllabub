LEARNING MODES:

This is a hybrid class incorporating classroom time in Bremerton. We will spend time together in the ground-based classroom and online using the Canvas Learning Management System. Curriculum will be delivered in a highly interactive format via lecture, demonstration, discussions (both online and in class), videos, small group projects, and so on. Assignments, Projects, Discussions, Quizzes, and Exams must be submitted through Canvas.

 

It is expected that the student will have a basic knowledge of the use of a microcomputer. This course is NOT an introduction to basic computer literacy. A basic understanding of and manipulation of the windows interface is expected. In particular, students should know how to use Windows Explorer to manipulate (save, copy, delete, move, etc.) files, have access to the Internet, know how to use your browser, fill out Web forms, use email (including attachments), and similar basic Web skills. If you do not feel comfortable with these basic skills, it is recommended that you consider taking CIS 107 and/or CIS 150 prior to taking this class to build computer literacy. Please have an alternate plan in case you have issues with your normal Internet access and related software that might affect your ability to participate in this class.

ATTENDANCE AND PARTICIPATION:

This is a very fast-paced course. Attendance and success in this class have a strong correlation. New concepts tend to build on previous concepts rather than stand on their own. If you have to miss a class I suggest you network with other students to gather notes. You are responsible for all information covered during normally scheduled class sessions, whether you are present or not.

 

Class sessions will be recorded and available for review, notwithstanding any technical difficulties. That said, attendance at the class sessions serves as a valuable addition to your learning experience. The recordings are supplied to aid in review of the material, and will generally be available on Canvas within 24 hours of the course session being recorded.

 

It is expected that students will be tolerant of others' views, be respectful in dealing with others, and use standard English in their communications, both written and verbal. Disruptive behavior will not be tolerated. Please review Olympic College's WAC 132C-120 policies. The various WACs have been passed by the state of Washington and are Washington State law that govern the behavior of the administration, faculty, and students.

 

POLICIES:

Unit assignments should be turned in on or before their due date. Pacing yourself based upon this schedule will help ensure your best success with the class. Also, you have four items due the last day of lectures I highly recommend you spread them out over the term and turn them in during term, instead of waiting until the end to do so.

 

It is okay to miss a due date for general labs and quizzeslate assignments will be accepted up to the final class lecture. This means you may NOT be late on the Midterm or Final exams, nor for the Article Treatment, Chapter Analysis, nor for Projects 3 and 4. All work must be complete by the final lecture. I do NOT recommend being late, as the pace of the class makes it very difficult to catch up. If you have special circumstances that impact your ability to turn assignments in when due, please communicate those to me through Canvas messaging right away.

If you wish to withdraw from this course, please inform me of your decision and file the appropriate paperwork with the Office of Registration and Records. If I have no data/grades to work with, an F will be given unless you have been in contact with me previously to make arrangements for an Incomplete; my understanding is that this has an impact upon Veteran funding.
